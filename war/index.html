COE Price Trends

Source: Land Transport Authority, Results of Bidding Exercises for Certificates of Entitlement. Chart omits prices for Category D (Motorcycles). Data available since 2008. Download the spreadsheet.

Despite the introduction of cooling measures by curbing motor vehicle loans in February 2013, COE prices are converging upwards across all categories, except motorcycles. COE for commercial vehicles (Category C) hit a record-high of $71,001 in August 2013. This will place a strain on small-to-medium enterprises (SMEs), such as school bus operators.

COE Quotas

Source: Land Transport Authority, Results of Bidding Exercises for Certificates of Entitlement. Data available since 2008. Download the spreadsheet.

Feast or Famine

  • "Feast" in yesteryears - From January to September 2008, the Category A quota exceeded 2,000 for each bidding exercise. If the current Vehicle Quota System (VQS) is left unchanged, this would also mean that the next bumper crop of COEs will be due in 2018, as the 2008 batch of COEs expires.
  • Now in the "Famine" cycle - The Category A quota has since trickled down to 300+ from November 2012 onwards.
  • The Motor Traders Association (MTA) also noted the "feast or famine" cycle in its letter to LTA.

Source: Land Transport Authority, Results of Bidding Exercises for Certificates of Entitlement. Current year in the chart represents the annual quota released so far. Data available since 2008. Download the spreadsheet.

Quotas are declining across all categories except motorcycles. The quota "famine" is pushing up the annual maximum COE price y-o-y. Although the annual average COE price dips in some years, the general price trend is upward.

Oversubscription

Source: Land Transport Authority, Results of Bidding Exercises for Certificates of Entitlement. Subscription rate is the ratio of the number of submitted bids to the number of quota available in the bidding exercise. Data available since 2008. Download the spreadsheet.

After regulatory restrictions on motor vehicle loans were introduced in February 2013, subscription rates actually exceeded normal levels for all categories except commercial vehicles and motorcycles. Why is that so?
